Hanabi rules

    There are 5 colors (red, green, blue, yellow, white) in the deck. In each color there are 10 cards: 3 cards are «1», 2 cards are «2», «3» and «4» and the last one is «5». Also, there are 3 lives and 8 hints.

    Hanabi is a collective game. All players have one goal: to maximize the global score – the amount of cards laid in solitaire. You can see all of the cards in the hands of other players, but not in yours. If there are 2 or 3 players, then each player has 5 cards, if there are 4 players, then each has 4 cards.

    Players make moves one by one. At every move player has to make one of the three actions: to throw one of his cards to junk, to add one of his cards to solitaire or to make a hint to another player.

    Junk: if a card goes to junk then it can't be used anymore. If the deck is not empty yet, then the player takes a new card. Also, if there are less then 8 hints left, it increases by one.

    Solitaire: a card goes to solitaire only if there already are cards of the same color and previous value (or empty for «1»). Otherwise, the card goes to junk and the player loses one life. You have only 3 lives. Also, if the added card is «5» then one hint is added.

    Hint: There are two types of hints: color hint and value hint. During each of your moves (if there is at least one hint chip left) you can choose to whom and what you want to hint (value or color) and after you've chosen you hint to your partner where all the cards with the same property are placed. For example, you can show where all the red cards in one hand are, or all the «2». After each hint one hint chip burns.

    The game ends if there are all the 25 cards in the solitaire, or if there are no lives left anymore, or if there are no cards in the deck and one circle of moves has been made.

    The result of the game is the amount of cards in the solitaire if there is at least one life left and otherwise – 0.
